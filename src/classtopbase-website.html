ClassTopBase Website

A marketing website for a school management software

Overview

ClassTopBase is a school management software for after-schools. It allows school to manage their students, classes, teacher. I joined the team last year as product designer. After a year long developing, we have way more features and the UI of the app got an overhaul. So It’s time to update the website to reflect on what we did last year.

Sitemaps

This is my first draft of sitemap. I want to show different case studies on how different kinds of school use our product to help them. However, our customer are not very diverse and they are just not fully interested in doing case studies. So I get rid of the case study.

Wireframe and Content

When I was doing wireframes, I encountered a great webinar by Pedro Cortés talked about how to create landing page that converts. He explained eight steps to create the content for the landing page.

  1. 1. Positioning your product
  2. 2. Headline
  3. 3. Introduction the big problem
  4. 4. Explain your solution
  5. 5. Mind the switching cost
  6. 6. Real social proof
  7. 7. “Final” CTA
  8. 8. Alternative CTA.

Because his advices, I think the home page content become more convicing and accurate.

Design

When I design the home page, I tried two different version. One is using collages to convey the idea of by using ClassTopBase, things will be more organized. Other one showed simple graphics on what each functions are. I decided on the latter as final result, because after asking couple people who are not tech savvy, the latter is easier to understand the the feel better connection between the content and the copy text. Although, in the future I think I will do a A/B tasting to try on whether which one is better.

Building

When building this website, I talked with my developer to use TailwindCSS as the css framework. Many people online said TailwindCSS is easy to use and save time. So when I design the website, I will use TailwindCSS variables. It will be easier for developers to translate the design into actual site.

Go Live

The site went live, but there are more things I want to do. Due to time and shift of our company main goals, this product is less important. So unfortunately I don’t have to time to try things I want to use. For examples, animations on the graphics. Insuring of using screenshot, I want to do the same style of graphics for every page.
